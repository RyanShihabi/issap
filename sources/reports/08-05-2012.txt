ISS On-Orbit Status 08/05/12
 
All ISS systems continue to function nominally, except those noted previously or below.    Sunday – Crew off duty.  Ahead:  Week 6 of Increment 32 (six-person crew).
 
At wakeup, Gennady Padalka performed the routine inspection of the SM (Service Module) PSS Caution &amp; Warning panel as part of regular Daily Morning Inspection.
 
Sergei Revin completed the routine daily &amp; weekly servicing of the SOZh system (Environment Control &amp; Life Support System, ECLSS) in the SM and FGB.     [This included the weekly collection of the toilet flush (SP) counter and water supply (SVO) readings of SM &amp; FGB for calldown to TsUP-Moscow, as well as the weekly checkup on the Russian POTOK-150MK (150 micron) air filter unit of the SM’s &amp; FGB’s SOGS air revitalization subsystem, gathering weekly data on total operating time &amp; “On” durations for calldown.  SOZh servicing includes checking the ASU toilet facilities, replacement of the KTO &amp; KBO solid waste containers and replacement of EDV-SV waste water and EDV-U urine containers as required.]
 
Suni Williams closed the protective shutters of the Lab, Node-3/Cupola &amp; JPM (JEM Pressurized Module) windows in preparation for tonight’s planned HTV3 EP (H-II Transfer Vehicle 3 Exposed Platform) hand-off by SSRMS (Space Station Remote Manipulator) to the JEM RMS (Robotic Maneuvering System).
 
At ~5:40am, Padalka, Revin &amp; Malenchenko joined for a PAO TV downlink, extending greetings &amp; congratulations for the celebration of the 100<sup>th</sup> Anniversary of Russia’s Air Force (8/12). 
 
At ~6:05am, Sunita powered up the SM's amateur radio equipment (Kenwood VHF transceiver with manual frequency selection, headset, &amp; power supply) and at 6:20am conducted a ham radio session with participants in Space Jam 6 at the Octave Chanute Aerospace Museum.
 
FE-2, FE-3 &amp; FE-6 conducted their weekly PFCs (Private Family Conferences), via S-band/audio and Ku-band/MS-NetMeeting application (which displays the uplinked ground video on an SSC laptop), Aki at ~3:45am, Sergei at ~7:40am, Joe at ~3:20pm EDT.
 
The crew worked out on the CEVIS cycle ergometer with vibration isolation (FE-3, FE-6), TVIS treadmill with vibration isolation &amp; stabilization (CDR, FE-2, FE-4), ARED advanced resistive exerciser (FE-2, FE-3, FE-5, FE-6), T2/COLBERT advanced treadmill (FE-5), and VELO ergometer bike with load trainer (CDR, FE-4).
 
Suni recorded her session on the T2/COLBERT advanced treadmill with the G1 camera and MPC (Multi-Protocol Converter) downlink.
